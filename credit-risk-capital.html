Credit Risk & Capital Management

NCGCL Credit Risk & Capital Management Framework

A practical intranet guide for risk-taking, capital allocation, pricing, portfolio monitoring, stress testing and Board reporting. The framework operationalizes NCGCL’s core principle: unlock credit through controlled risk-sharing while preserving capital, maintaining AAA resilience and ensuring every guarantee is priced and governed by risk.

Policy Intent

What the Framework Does

🤝
Principle

Risk-sharing, not risk transfer

NCGCL catalyzes credit by sharing a defined part of credit risk with PFIs. PFIs must retain meaningful exposure in every transaction to preserve underwriting discipline, monitoring effort and recovery incentives.

Use this as the first test for every product design.
🏛️
Solvency

AAA resilience as product value

The framework treats NCGCL’s credit standing as central to its guarantee value proposition. Risk decisions must therefore be evaluated through their effect on solvency, capital adequacy and stakeholder confidence.

Economic capital should be aligned with a high-confidence solvency posture.
💰
Capital

Core vs. Risk Capital

Core Capital is ring-fenced to absorb unexpected losses. Risk Capital is the deployable surplus that supports guarantee issuance, subject to Internal CAR, leverage and exposure limits.

Guarantee capacity is based on Risk Capital, not total capital.
🌐
Overlay

Systemic risk overlay

Quarterly overlays allow management and the GC to tighten limits, impose pricing surcharges, or pause high-risk segments when macro, political, sovereign, currency or banking-sector risk deteriorates.

Designed to make risk appetite forward-looking.
Operating Model

Credit Risk Lifecycle

1
Set Appetite
Board-approved risk appetite defines leverage, concentration, RAROC and Internal CAR thresholds.
2
Estimate PD/LGD/EAD
Assign probability of default, loss given default and exposure at default to every obligor, pool or instrument.
3
Choose Structure
Select pari passu, first-loss or second-loss structure based on risk-sharing, capital intensity and development objective.
4
Allocate Capital
Calculate expected loss, unexpected loss, economic capital and impact on Internal CAR and leverage capacity.
5
Price Risk
Guarantee fee = Expected Loss + Capital Charge + Admin Cost Load, calibrated through target RAROC.
6
Monitor & Stress
Track KRIs, HHI, watchlists, RAROC, ECL staging, stress losses and GC/Board actions.

Capital efficiency principle
Pari passu structures are the default scalable risk-sharing product; second-loss structures are most capital-efficient where PFIs can absorb first loss; first-loss structures should be capped, priced materially higher and reserved for strategically important inclusion or pilot programs.
Pricing

Risk-Based Pricing Model

Annual Guarantee Fee

Annual Guarantee Fee = Expected Loss + Capital Charge + Administrative Cost Load

  • Expected Loss: the actuarial cost of risk based on PD, LGD and EAD.
  • Capital Charge: Economic Capital × target RAROC.
  • Admin Cost Load: underwriting, monitoring, legal, operations and portfolio management cost recovery.

Dynamic Recalibration

Pricing assumptions should be reviewed annually against actual loss experience. Underperforming sectors, PFIs or products should receive upward PD/LGD recalibration or pricing surcharge; outperforming segments may be moderated subject to development impact and Board risk appetite.

Single Sector

Exposure to any single economic sector is capped at 20% of the outstanding guarantee portfolio.

High-risk sectors such as speculative real estate are capped at 5% unless approved mitigants exist.

PFI

Single PFI

Exposure to any single PFI or related group is capped at 25% of total contingent guarantee portfolio.

Risk Mitigation

Mitigation Tools Embedded in the Framework

🧾
PFI

PFI Counterparty Scorecard

Weighted assessment across financial strength, governance and operational capacity. Active PFIs should be re-scored annually and linked to exposure limits and monitoring intensity.

Weights: Financial Strength 40%, Governance 30%, Operational Capacity 30%.
🏗️
Security

Collateral & Funded Reserves

Collateral quality reduces LGD. For high-risk pilots, funded first-loss reserves from PFIs or development partners can materially reduce NCGCL capital consumption.

Focus: enforceability, valuation, control and claim timing.
🛡️
Tail Risk

Counter-guarantees & Reinsurance

As the book grows, counter-guarantees or reinsurance can transfer part of tail risk and create additional capital relief.

Best suited for high-correlation or catastrophe-like exposures.
🌦️
Emerging

Climate Risk Integration

Develop sectoral heatmaps for transition risk and geographic maps for physical risk hotspots. Integrate climate due diligence into PFI and portfolio monitoring.

Future state: climate scenarios in stress testing.
🔍
Monitoring

Early Intervention

Pre-defined triggers should prompt enhanced PFI reporting, tightened underwriting, remedial action plans or temporary suspension of new guarantees in stressed segments.

Monthly management reporting; quarterly GC reporting.
💧
Liquidity

Liquidity & Claims Readiness

Capital must be invested prudently so that legitimate claims can be paid promptly, maintaining trust with PFIs while preserving solvency.

Claims process should be transparent, documented and auditable.

Desk Reference

Quick Checklists

Before approving a guarantee

Confirm PFI eligibility, borrower/program PD and LGD, sector/PFI/program headroom, guarantee structure, PFI retained risk, pricing adequacy, legal enforceability and post-deal CAR/leverage impact.

Before recommending a first-loss structure

Confirm development rationale, why pari passu or second-loss is insufficient, funded reserve availability, 100% capital treatment, higher fee, tighter covenants and explicit GC/Board visibility.

Monthly risk pack

Include exposure vs limits, Internal CAR, leverage, HHI by sector/PFI/region, watchlist migration, past-due trend, claims/recoveries, RAROC by product and PFI reporting quality.

Quarterly GC / Board pack

Include systemic risk overlay, stress scenarios, limit breaches, pricing recalibration issues, top concentrations, Stage 2/Stage 3 movements, emerging climate/geographic risks and required policy actions.

Annual model review

Back-test PD/LGD assumptions, loss experience, admin cost load, capital intensity by structure, ECL staging triggers, pricing matrix and portfolio diversification assumptions.

Data quality checks

Validate obligor ID, PFI ID, sector, region, guarantee amount, outstanding principal, arrears status, collateral, recoveries, claim status and all fields used in ECL and capital calculations.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Because NCGCL is designed as a risk-sharing institution, not a risk-transfer vehicle. If PFIs do not retain meaningful exposure, they may have weaker incentives to underwrite, monitor and recover loans properly. The 75% maximum cover preserves at least 25% PFI skin-in-the-game.
Core Capital is a strategic reserve for unexpected losses and claims under severe stress. It protects NCGCL’s solvency and AAA-style resilience, so it is not available for routine growth or new guarantee issuance.
Expected Loss is the average predictable credit loss and should be covered through guarantee fees and provisions. Economic Capital covers unexpected loss volatility and therefore determines how much Risk Capital is consumed by the transaction.
First-loss guarantees absorb the earliest and most probable losses. The policy therefore treats them as highly capital-intensive, generally requiring capital equal to 100% of the guaranteed cap. This makes the capital charge and required fee materially higher than pari passu or second-loss structures.
HHI should be calculated monthly for PFI, sector and regional concentration. It is not a replacement for hard exposure caps; it supplements them by detecting whether portfolio exposure is becoming concentrated even before a single hard limit is breached.
Overlays should be considered when macroeconomic, sovereign, political, currency, banking-sector or sector-specific conditions deteriorate. Possible actions include pricing surcharges, tighter limits, lower coverage, higher collateral requirements or a temporary pause in new guarantees.
Bottom line
The Credit Risk & Capital Management Framework is the engine that converts NCGCL’s development mandate into disciplined, measurable and Board-governed risk-taking. The operating discipline is simple: identify risk, quantify PD/LGD/EAD, choose the right structure, allocate capital, price through RAROC, monitor concentration and intervene early when portfolio signals deteriorate.
