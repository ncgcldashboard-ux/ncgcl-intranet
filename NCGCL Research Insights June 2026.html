Working Hypothesis

In Pakistan, financing constraints frequently coexist with, and are sometimes downstream of, architecture constraints. Logistics, settlement, custody, processing, energy, market access, regulation. Research follows where credit is binding, where it is not, and where guarantee products should wait until the underlying architecture has matured. Two more workstreams graduated this month: post-harvest storage infrastructure, and the missing middle of the export value chain.

Doctrine · Risk Architecture

The four layers of the loss stack

Effective guarantee systems position NCGCL in the guarantee layer, above the lender deductible, below unexpected loss and catastrophic tail risk. The lender retains first loss. NCGCL covers a defined window of eligible losses. Capital reserve and sovereign backstops absorb what sits above. Headline coverage ratios are a weak proxy; effective exposure is set by caps, deductibles, exclusions, recoveries, and claim discipline. ASIFF's PFI-senior structure is the clearest current application of the doctrine.

Catalytic first-loss capital

Internal: Strategy · Capital Markets · Board

Sector Doctrine · Microfinance

Microfinance strategy

Internal: Product · Origination

Prioritise second-layer or pari passu risk-sharing. Lenders retain ordinary expected loss; NCGCL shares the unexpected-loss layer in productive, data-rich, portfolio pilots, not disguised consumption smoothing.

Tail-risk caveat: Pakistani microfinance has seen highly correlated stress (2008–12, COVID, 2022 floods). Insulate via product-window discipline.

Policy · Stabilisation

Wheat market transition

Internal: Strategy · Risk · Govt Relations

Reform without transition is market shock. If the state no longer buys wheat, it must enable someone else to hold it. Sovereign-backed put options are fiscally cheap when unused, explosive when triggered. Explicit fiscal sizing required.

Pilot Architecture
5.1 · HBL Zarai · Bundled Execution

Portfolio risk-sharing for deferred trade-credit receivables

Custody-Based Recovery · Controlled Sandbox

Credit cover is the enabling layer inside a bundled execution model: inputs, agronomy, mechanisation, procurement, off-take, in-kind settlement, custody-based recovery. Cover applies to eligible crystallised net losses after recoveries; weak origination, custody failure, and side-selling are excluded.

5.3 · ASIFF · Post-Procurement Architecture

Agricultural Storage Infrastructure Finance Facility

Prepared for IFC Structuring & Investment Teams · Phase I Anchor 1.0 MMT

Addresses the commercial storage and inventory-financing gap facing millers, processors, exporters, and traders, distinct from smallholder liquidity needs. Phase I: ~67 standard warehouses, Rs 20.16 Bn PFI senior debt, 60% LTV, 7-year tenor, 1.25× target DSCR. NCGCL positioned as principal guarantor and single national wheat-credit guarantee counterparty.

Investment thesis: storage is inventory management, not price arbitrage. Near-import-parity production costs remove the seasonal carry trade.

Programme Brief
5.4 · NEAGAP · June 2026

National Export-Oriented Agricultural Finance Guarantee Programme

Sponsoring Ministry: MNFS&R · Executing Partner: NCGCL

A partial credit guarantee window inside NCGCL, not a lending or subsidy vehicle. PKR 1 Bn public risk capital matched by PKR 1 Bn NCGCL second-loss capital, each leveraged 3×, generates PKR 6 Bn guarantee capacity at 25% coverage. PFI retains the residual 75%. Targets PKR 24 Bn in mobilised bank financing for the missing middle beyond the farm gate: aggregation, cold chain, processing, grading, logistics.

Portfolio discipline: core window ≥70% established enterprises, first-mover window ≤30%. Target: 160–200 enterprises financed, ≥15,000 jobs supported over five years. NCGCL's second-loss participation remains subject to shareholder approval.

Open questions

Surfaced by the research pipeline. For continued stakeholder reflection, not items requiring formal direction at this stage.

Open
Q1 · Instrument Class · Governance

Catalytic first-loss: perimeter & insulation

As the framework develops, the perimeter, eligibility, and approval architecture warrant continued reflection, particularly on insulating the instrument from absorption into the standard guarantee product set.

Open
Q2 · Carrying Capacity · Floor Credibility

Wheat transition: NCGCL's optimal role

Credit enhancement over private carrying capacity is the natural starting point. Whether complementary risk-bearing structures could materially strengthen private absorption warrants reflection.

Open
Q3 · Sequencing · Two Facilities, One Counterparty

ASIFF and WLPSF: coordination as NCGCL's single-counterparty role scales

NCGCL sits as principal guarantor across both the smallholder liquidity facility and the commercial storage facility. How exposure, governance, and reporting are kept distinct across two structurally different instruments sharing one guarantor warrants continued reflection.

Open
Q4 · Pilot Discipline · Shareholder Governance

NEAGAP: scaling discipline once public risk capital is confirmed

NEAGAP is structured as a ring-fenced guarantee window with PKR 1 Bn public risk capital matched by PKR 1 Bn NCGCL second-loss capacity. NCGCL's second-loss participation remains subject to shareholder approval: the sequencing between ministry confirmation and NCGCL board sign-off warrants reflection.
