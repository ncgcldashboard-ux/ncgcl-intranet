Policy Intent

What the Guarantee Policy Does

Core purpose

The policy defines how NCGCL can provide partial credit guarantees to lenders, financiers, and eligible debt instrument holders to catalyze financing for SMEs, agriculture, and underserved borrowers while preserving NCGCL’s capital base.

  • Share credit risk with Partner Financial Institutions instead of replacing their underwriting discipline.
  • Promote access to finance in underserved segments while maintaining manageable exposure.
  • Use defined exposure limits, eligibility rules, due diligence, monitoring, and approval tiers.
Golden Rule
A guarantee should create additional lending while ensuring that the PFI retains meaningful risk, performs proper underwriting, monitors the borrower, and pursues recovery before claim settlement.
Objective
🚀

Unlock Additional Credit

Use guarantees to make viable but underserved borrowers bankable, especially where risk perception, collateral limitations, or sector unfamiliarity constrain lending.

Control
🏦

Preserve Capital

Maintain exposure discipline through caps by program, sector, PFI, issuer, and risk rating so guarantee growth remains sustainable.

Portfolio
📊

Diversify Risk

Avoid concentration in one sector, one PFI, one product program, or volatile segments by applying hard limits and ongoing portfolio reviews.

Governance
⚖️

Escalate by Risk

Use tiered approval levels so larger, riskier, longer tenor, or more complex guarantees receive Committee or Board scrutiny.

Guarantee Coverage

Coverage Bands and Structuring Logic

Coverage is risk-based. Stronger borrowers, stronger cash flows, better collateral, lower-risk sectors, and stronger PFI alignment should generally require lower coverage. Higher coverage should be reserved for strategically important but riskier segments, with stronger mitigation.

Who Qualifies

Borrower and PFI Eligibility

Borrowers
🏢

Borrower Eligibility Checklist

Borrowers should normally have at least two years of operational history, provide audited financial statements for the previous two years or acceptable projections / management accounts where permitted, undergo credit assessment through internal scoring or rating, and operate in sectors NCGCL is willing to support.

Smaller entities and cash-flow financing programs may require tailored documentation rather than full audited statements.
PFIs
🏛️

PFI Eligibility Checklist

PFIs must be licensed and compliant, demonstrate strong underwriting and risk management, maintain diversified lending, submit regular performance reports, and preferably hold at least a BB+ local credit rating. NCGCL also reviews CAR, asset quality, liquidity, profitability, governance, reputation, and recovery capability.

PFI onboarding requires Guarantee Committee approval regardless of initial exposure.
Due Diligence

How Risk Is Assessed

The policy deliberately moves beyond collateral-only lending. Assessment should combine borrower financial strength, cash-flow resilience, value-chain position, collateral/LGD, sector dynamics, and PFI counterparty quality.

Sector
🌐

Sector Risk

Assess market volatility, cyclicality, regulatory risk, and environmental/social risk before setting coverage and exposure.

Cash Flow
💧

Cash-Flow Strength

Review stability, conservative projections, prospective DSCR preferably above 1.2x, receivable/payable cycle, working capital, and liquidity ratios.

Value Chain
🔗

Value-Chain Position

Evaluate supplier dependency, customer concentration, market share, pricing power, industry direction, and long-term contracts.

Collateral
🔐

Collateral & LGD

Assess collateral quality and enforceability. LGD assumptions should follow policy annexures and be reflected in pricing, coverage, and approvals.

PFI Risk
🏦

PFI Counterparty Risk

Evaluate the lender’s underwriting, loan monitoring, recovery track record, financial stability, and portfolio quality before extending exposure.

Scoring
🎯

Coverage Scoring

Credit score, cash-flow strength, and value-chain position should guide the coverage level and whether additional mitigants are required.

Default & Recovery Principle
When a borrower defaults, the PFI must notify NCGCL immediately. NCGCL reviews the default, collaborates on a recovery plan, and honors the guarantee payout only after recovery avenues have been pursued and the remaining default is established under the relevant documentation.
Debt Market Credit Enhancement

Capital Market Instruments

The policy permits guarantees for capital market instruments, but this exposure is deliberately ring-fenced and subject to more specific controls because it introduces issuer, market, duration, liquidity, and instrument-structure risks.

Eligible
📜

Eligible Instruments

Corporate bonds, commercial papers, and other structured or asset-backed debt securities may be considered if they meet NCGCL’s risk and creditworthiness criteria.

Criteria
🔎

Evaluation Criteria

Review issuer creditworthiness, instrument rating, use of proceeds, underlying cash flows, tenor/structure, collateral, and exchange listing with transparent price discovery.

Limits
📈

Coverage & Caps

Coverage may range from 10% to 50%, total capital-market exposure may not exceed 10% of contingent liabilities, and each issuer is limited to 20% of the capital-market sub-limit.

Quick Checklists

Before a Guarantee Approval Memo

Confirm eligibility, sector limit, PFI limit, coverage band, borrower cash flows, DSCR, collateral/LGD, pricing, legal documentation, conditions precedent, and approval authority.

Before PFI Onboarding

Check license, rating, CAR, NPLs, provisioning, liquidity, profitability, governance, internal audit, underwriting, monitoring, recovery history, and reporting capability.

For Quarterly Portfolio Review

Report exposure against program, sector, PFI, issuer, and instrument limits; summarize past-dues, watchlist, defaults, recoveries, claims, pricing adequacy, and risk profile changes.

For Capital Market Instruments

Verify instrument eligibility, issuer creditworthiness, instrument rating, use-of-proceeds alignment, cash-flow coverage, tenor, listing status, liquidity, collateral, and issuer sub-limit.

Common Questions

FAQ

For ordinary loan guarantees, maximum coverage can go up to 75% of the financing amount, depending on borrower, sector, program, and risk profile. For capital market instruments, coverage ranges from 10% to 50%.
Board approval is required for guarantees exceeding PKR 1 billion or greater than 20% of total equity, whichever is lower, and for the highest-risk or most complex transactions.
The policy prefers PFIs with a minimum BB+ credit rating. Cumulative exposure to unrated PFIs may be restricted to 10% of NCGCL equity and should be tightly governed.
The PFI must notify NCGCL immediately on default. NCGCL reviews the default and collaborates on recovery. Guarantee payout is honored only after recovery avenues have been explored and the remaining default is established under the relevant documentation.
