Why it matters

Purpose of the Framework

Mission-aligned measurement for catalytic finance

The Impact Assessment Framework is designed to systematically measure, manage and report NCGCL’s progress against its strategic objectives. It gives the organization a disciplined way to understand whether guarantees are translating into economic productivity, SME growth, climate action and inclusion.

  • Measures both development impact and operational delivery.
  • Creates accountability to Board, regulators, funding partners and the public.
  • Supports evidence-based adaptation of products, strategy and operations.
  • Ensures impact claims are grounded in verifiable data rather than anecdotes.
Core Question
🎯

Are we achieving our mission?

The framework tracks whether NCGCL is enabling additional finance and whether that finance is supporting revenue growth, jobs, emissions reduction, women/youth inclusion and stronger institutional performance.

Practical implementation

Quick Checklists

Before issuing a new product/program

Define applicable impact indicators, baseline data requirement, PFI reporting fields, climate/inclusion tags, survey feasibility and expected reporting burden.

Before accepting PFI data

Check completeness, format consistency, definitions, sector tagging, beneficiary classification, duplicate records and evidence availability.

Before Board reporting

Reconcile internal guarantee data with PFI submissions, validate outliers, separate provisional from verified figures and document assumptions.

Before publishing impact claims

Confirm methodology, avoid over-attribution, disclose data limitations, use conservative language and maintain audit trail for figures.

For climate finance

Classify projects against a recognized taxonomy, use recognized GHG protocols, document emission factors and retain calculation worksheets.

For inclusion finance

Apply clear criteria for women/youth-led SMEs, retain evidence basis, and disaggregate by ownership, management and beneficiary category where possible.
