How to Read This Page

Decision Architecture

BOD
🏛️

Board of Directors

Approves budgets, material write-offs/disposals, operating-to-capital reallocations, overdrafts/loans, EVP positions, financial statements, auditor remuneration, operational manuals, policy amendments, major guarantees, capital/risk appetite and out-of-policy exceptions.

Committee
👥

Board / Advisory Committees

Board committees and advisory committees review delegated matters. The Guarantee Committee approves guarantees within its tier and can impose dynamic risk constraints. MIC is a management committee for investment decisions within the IPS.

CEO
✅

Chief Executive Officer

Executes day-to-day authority across bank accounts, liquid investments, LC issuance within budget, bank guarantees, contracts, legal correspondence, operational matters, within-budget reallocations, and lower-tier approvals.

Escalation
🚩

Stricter Rule Prevails

Where the matrix and a functional policy overlap, the stricter threshold should prevail. Breaches of exposure limits, Internal CAR, leverage, investment constraints or guarantee coverage caps require escalation and documented rationale.

Guarantee Authorities

Guarantee Approval & Exposure Delegations

CEO
🛡️

Lower-Risk Guarantees

CEO may approve guarantees up to PKR 100 million or 10% of the relevant capital base, whichever is lower. CEO approvals should be reported to the Guarantee Committee at its next meeting.

Guarantee Committee
📋

Committee Tier

Guarantee Committee / BGC may approve guarantees up to PKR 1 billion or 20% of relevant capital, whichever is lower, and guarantees requiring more than 50% coverage within its limit.

BOD
🏛️

Board Tier

Board approval is required for guarantees above PKR 1 billion, above 20% of capital, highest-risk/complex deals, deviations, multi-lender transactions and >50% coverage exceeding BGC limits.

Investment Authorities

Investment Policy & Treasury Delegations

BOD
💼

Policy & Oversight

Board approves the IPS, reviews quarterly performance, receives breach reporting, approves equity exposure if introduced, and approves material changes to investment policy or risk appetite.

MIC
📈

Management Investment Committee

MIC meets weekly and directs investment management within the IPS. Quorum is three members, with CEO and CIO/Fund Manager mandatory. Decisions are by simple majority and CEO has casting vote.

CEO
🏦

Execution Authority

Deposits and liquid investments are delegated to the CEO under the DoA, but must remain within IPS constraints, MIC process, banking limits, credit quality limits and AAA-preservation requirements.

Capital & Risk Delegations

Credit Risk, Capital Management & Risk Appetite

BOD
🎯

Risk Appetite

Board approves the Risk Appetite Statement, capital allocation limits, policy amendments and the capital posture required to preserve AAA resilience.

BOD
🧱

Core Capital

Core Capital is ring-fenced for unexpected losses and claims under severe stress. It is recalculated annually by Risk Management and approved by the Board.

GC
🌐

Risk Overlay Levers

GC may tighten sector limits, pause new guarantees in risky segments, impose pricing surcharge such as 25 bps, or apply overlays during systemic stress.

Hard Stop
⛔

CAR / Leverage Breach

If Internal CAR falls below 20% or nominal leverage cap is breached, issuance must halt or materially slow until capital is restored or Board-approved action is taken.

Governance Hygiene

Control Notes & Clean-up Items

Review
🧭

Procurement Threshold Alignment

The uploaded DoA marks “Macro Purchases up to PKR 1 million” as Board-approved, while “Approval of Purchase Orders up to and above PKR 1 million” is marked CEO. For operational clarity, the next DoA revision should distinguish procurement method approval, vendor selection approval, and PO execution authority.

Gap
🧩

PKR 1m–20m Procurement Band

The matrix explicitly shows full and open competition above PKR 20 million, but does not clearly define the authority for competitive procurements between PKR 1 million and PKR 20 million. This should be mapped to procurement policy and committee approvals.

Escalate
🔁

Policy-Embedded Limits

Guarantee, investment and capital management limits are not merely operational thresholds. Breaches affect NCGCL’s AAA posture and solvency. Any exception should carry policy reference, risk impact, capital impact and explicit approval by the appropriate committee or Board.

Document
📝

Sub-Delegation Trail

The CEO may sub-delegate non-financial powers. Each sub-delegation should be written, role-based, time-bound and recorded in a central authority register. Financial authority should not be sub-delegated unless expressly approved.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

If the CEO can approve a bank deposit, does MIC approval still matter?+
Yes. The DoA delegates execution authority for deposits and liquid investments to the CEO, while the IPS establishes the MIC as the weekly investment decision forum. The practical route should be MIC decision within IPS limits, followed by CEO-authorized execution.
Which authority approves a guarantee above PKR 100 million?+
A guarantee above the CEO tier routes to the Guarantee Committee / BGC if it is within PKR 1 billion or 20% of relevant capital, whichever is lower. Anything above that, or highest-risk/complex/deviation cases, goes to the Board.
What if the amount is within a threshold but breaches a sector, PFI, CAR, leverage or IPS limit?+
Escalate. Amount thresholds are only one layer. Limit breaches require GC/Board review, and in some cases issuance or execution must pause until the breach is remediated or approved at the appropriate level.
Can the CEO sub-delegate financial authority?+
No. The uploaded matrix allows the CEO to sub-delegate powers delegated by the Board to employees, excluding financial authority. Non-financial sub-delegations should be documented in writing.
What is the safest escalation rule where policies appear inconsistent?+
Use the more conservative authority route, document the inconsistency, and escalate to the relevant committee or Board if the matter is material. Maintain a DoA change log for periodic clean-up.
Operating Principle
The Delegation of Authority Matrix should be used as a routing map, not as a substitute for judgment. For NCGCL, the most important control principle is that operational speed must remain consistent with capital preservation, AAA resilience, risk-sharing discipline, and auditable decision-making. The correct approver is therefore the highest authority required by any applicable policy, limit, risk condition or exception.
