Policy Intent

What the IPS is designed to achieve

Core principle: returns must never undermine capital safety.

The IPS gives NCGCL an investment framework for managing institutional assets with a long-term horizon, but with capital preservation, liquidity, post-tax return optimization, and AAA-rating protection as non-negotiable constraints.

  • Generate real growth of assets by targeting returns above inflation.
  • Preserve liquidity so guarantee obligations and operating needs can be met without stress.
  • Maintain conservative credit quality and avoid excessive duration, concentration or illiquidity.
  • Use tactical asset allocation only within approved limits and risk posture.
Management takeaway
NCGCL is not managing a return-maximization pool. It is managing institutional capital that supports its guarantee business, stakeholder confidence, and AAA rating. Investment yield is important, but only after capital preservation, liquidity and compliance gates are satisfied.

Fixed Income Risk Controls

Capital preservation controls for the portfolio

⏱️
Duration

Short-to-intermediate duration

No instrument should exceed 3 years in tenor. Portfolio average duration should not exceed 360 days.

Monitor: duration by instrument and portfolio
📉
Interest rate risk

PVBP discipline

Portfolio PVBP should not exceed 3 bps for every 1 bp movement in interest rates. Breaches require priority rectification and quarterly Board communication.

Monitor: PVBP and rate sensitivity
🏛️
Credit quality

75% high-grade anchor

At least 75% of the portfolio, including Core Capital, must be allocated to government securities, AAA instruments, AA+ bank placements, sovereign-focused money market funds, or AAA DFI placements.

Monitor: quality mix weekly
🏢
Corporate bucket

30% risk bucket

Up to 30% may be allocated to AA+ corporate fixed income, AA+ DFI placements within sub-limits, or income mutual funds.

Monitor: sector, issuer and liquidity

Counterparty & Fund Controls

Bank, DFI and mutual fund requirements

🏦
Bank placements

Only strong scheduled banks

Banks must be scheduled banks with minimum AA+ rating. No exposure is allowed to Microfinance Banks. Bank placements should be in savings/daily product accounts with competitive returns.

Non-remunerative/current accounts: minimal, max PKR 10m
📏
Exposure test

Bank exposure gates

NCGCL deposits should not exceed 2% of a bank's deposit base or 4% of a bank's equity base; CAR must exceed SBP minimum requirements.

Use: Counterparty approval gate
🏗️
DFI

DFI caps

AAA-rated DFIs are capped at 15% of portfolio; AA+ DFIs are capped at 10%. NCGCL placement should not exceed 10% of the DFI's equity base.

Use: DFI exposure review

Risk, Liquidity & Tax

Portfolio disciplines beyond allocation limits

🧪
Stress testing

Volatility management

Use stress testing, scenario analysis and VaR to assess portfolio risks under adverse market conditions. The Board should be apprised semi-annually.

Output: stress loss, liquidity impact, rating sensitivity
💧
Liquidity

Liquidity waterfall

Maintain minimum bank placements, use staggered maturities, and avoid over-concentration in less liquid corporate bonds and commercial paper.

Output: maturity ladder and liquidity buffer
🧾
Tax

After-tax return lens

Instrument selection must consider different tax treatments, timing of tax collection and annual tax code changes, while remaining within IPS limits.

Output: pre-tax vs post-tax yield comparison
⚖️
Compliance

Legal and regulatory compliance

The portfolio must comply with applicable laws, regulations and internal governance policies at all times.

Output: compliance certification
🗓️
Time horizon

Long horizon, liquid posture

NCGCL's 10+ year horizon permits measured risk tolerance, but capital preservation and AAA-rating support remain critical.

Output: strategic fit assessment
🚦
Exception

Breach management

Tolerance breaches should be rectified on priority. Credit-quality misalignment should be rectified within one quarter, and PVBP breaches reported to the Board quarterly.

Output: breach log and action owner

What should go to the Board

📊
Quarterly

Board investment dashboard

At minimum: portfolio return %, benchmark return %, instrument allocation, credit-quality allocation, portfolio duration and PVBP.

Owner: MIC / CFO / CIO
🧪
Semi-annual

Risk stress pack

Semi-annual Board update on stress testing, scenario analysis and VaR under relevant market conditions.

Owner: Risk / CIO
🔁
Review

IPS review cycle

Review annually for strategic alignment; during the first year, review semi-annually, and thereafter every two years unless conditions require earlier action.

Owner: Board / MIC
Suggested quarterly Board pack structure
Start with one page on portfolio performance versus benchmark and inflation; then show allocation by asset class and credit quality; summarize duration/PVBP and liquidity ladder; disclose all breaches/exceptions and corrective actions; highlight tax-adjusted yield opportunities; conclude with MIC outlook and proposed rebalancing actions.
Quick Reference

Checklists for daily use

Before approving any investment

Confirm instrument eligibility, minimum rating, asset-class headroom, issuer/counterparty concentration, duration impact, PVBP impact, liquidity impact, post-tax return, and compliance with current MIC/Board guidance.

Weekly MIC agenda

Review maturity ladder, expected cash needs, rate outlook, benchmark performance, allocation drift, credit-quality changes, tax-yield opportunities, breaches and proposed tactical shifts.

Monthly rebalancing

Compare actual allocation against SAA ranges, identify misalignment, update portfolio duration/PVBP, test counterparty limits, refresh tax-adjusted yield table, and document actions in MIC minutes.

Quarterly Board pack

Include portfolio return %, benchmark return %, CPI comparison, instrument allocation, credit-quality mix, duration, PVBP, liquidity profile, stress indicators, exceptions and corrective action status.

Bank placement checklist

Confirm scheduled bank status, minimum AA+ rating, deposit-base and equity-base limits, CAR above SBP minimum, product type, interest rate competitiveness, and current account balance cap.

Mutual fund checklist

Confirm AMC rating of AM1 or better, fund mandate, fund size, single AMC exposure, single fund exposure, asset class category, liquidity terms and tax treatment.
